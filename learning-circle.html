You have access to AI. So why isn't it working?

Maybe more access than you know what to do with — a work Copilot license you open twice a month, a ChatGPT subscription, a Claude tab, a folder of saved prompts you never reuse, and a browser full of articles about tools you haven't tried yet.

And still, most days, it isn't quite working. The outputs sound like everyone else's. You retype the same instructions from scratch because last week's version is lost in a chat history you'll never find. You get a draft that's eighty percent fine and quietly wrong in the twenty percent that matters. So you use AI for the small stuff, skip it for the work that counts, and carry a private, low-grade worry that everyone around you has figured out something you haven't.

Here is the truth the training industry keeps skipping: AI access is not AI readiness. Nobody handed you a method. They handed you a login.

The problem was never that you're behind. The problem is that you've been starting with the tool instead of the problem — experimenting without intention, chasing products, accepting first outputs, rebuilding the same instructions again and again. That's not a character flaw. It's exactly what happens when access outruns readiness.

And one more truth, the one that matters most: AI adoption is emotional before it is technical. The overwhelm, the skepticism, the quiet worry — that isn't weakness, and it isn't noise. Anxiety is not always an obstacle. Sometimes it is information the change strategy has ignored. This program treats it as information, and works with it.

What you'll learn — and what you'll build

You will learn capabilities and judgment — not dependence on any brand. What generative AI actually is and is not, and how it differs from search, automation, assistants, models, interfaces, and agents — so the vocabulary stops being intimidating and starts being useful. You'll learn to think in capabilities first and products second, so when the tools change (and they will), your method doesn't.

You'll learn to brief instead of prompt. A prompt gives a task. A brief gives direction. The SCRIPT™ methodology — Situation, Clear Intent, Role, Instructions, Parameters, Test — is distinctive for the two steps most frameworks skip: it begins by declaring your intent and ends by testing the output against that intent. It's the same briefing discipline that runs Grand Strategy's own business development.

You'll learn to verify. Model predicts. Human verifies. You'll build a working evaluation habit — how to test outputs, where AI is genuinely unreliable, and when not to use AI at all. Because the more capable AI becomes, the more accountable human judgment must become. And you'll work with the emotional side honestly, using Emotional Maths®: treating what you feel about AI as data about the change you're navigating, not a weakness to hide.

This is a building program, and you will leave with assets, not notes. By the final session: two to three functioning workflows built around problems you actually have, each with its verification step and its "when not to use AI" guardrail attached; a personal SCRIPT™ brief library so you never start from a blank prompt box again; a verification process you trust; a tool-selection framework that tells you which capability (and which subscription, if any) a task genuinely needs; and a 30-day implementation plan — a sustainable personal AI practice, not temporary enthusiasm.

The eight weeks at a glance

The program follows the SUMMS™ arc — See, Understand, Make, Manage, Sustain. You won't memorize the framework; you'll live inside it.

Week 1 · SEE

Take honest stock: where AI already touches your work, where your time actually goes, and what you feel about all of it — because feelings about AI are data, and we start there on purpose.

Week 2 · UNDERSTAND

Learn what generative AI is and is not — capabilities, limits, and the difference between search, automation, assistants, and agents — so you can stop chasing tools and start matching capability to problem.

Week 3 · SCRIPT

Stop prompting. Start briefing. Learn the full SCRIPT™ methodology — Situation, Clear Intent, Role, Instructions, Parameters, Test — and write your first real briefs for your real work.

Week 4 · MAKE

Put the method to work: produce your first AI-supported deliverable from an actual current responsibility, and evaluate it like a professional rather than accepting it like a first draft.

Week 5 · MANAGE

Build the judgment layer: verification habits, privacy and confidentiality rules, and the honest answer to "should AI touch this at all?"

Week 6 · BUILD

Turn one-off outputs into a reusable workflow — mapped, briefed, tested, and documented — chosen from a library seeded by workflows in live commercial use.

Week 7 · ORCHESTRATE

Move from one chatbot to a working system: connect your workflows, direct multiple capabilities, and take your place as the human in the lead.

Week 8 · SUSTAIN

Showcase your workflows to the cohort, lock in your 30-day plan, and leave with a practice built to outlast the program — and the next product cycle.

Why a Learning Circle — and not another AI course

Because you've probably already taken an AI webinar. And here you are.

One-off AI webinars rarely change behavior. You watch someone else's demo on someone else's problem, collect a PDF, feel briefly informed, and return to work exactly as you left it. The gap between watching and doing never closes, because nothing in the format asks you to do anything.

A Learning Circle is a different kind of room. Practice rooms, not webinars. Twelve to sixteen people, eight weeks, every session built around practice on your real problems — not canned examples. Live demonstrations use genuine working workflows, including the facilitator's own. You try things, some fail in front of people, and that's the point: the Circle has working agreements, confidentiality expectations, and a deliberate process for discussing unreliable outputs and false starts, because psychological safety isn't a nicety here — it's the mechanism that makes practice possible. Nobody dominates, nobody gets left behind, and skeptics are welcome; skepticism, handled honestly, is some of the best data in the room.

You also get what no recording can give you: peers building workflows next to you, an accountability partner who notices when you stall, and a final showcase where you present something that works.

Courses transfer information. Circles change practice. You've had enough information.

Questions, answered honestly

Do I need to be technical?

No. The program is designed for nontechnical professionals, and the curriculum explains capabilities in plain language before it ever mentions a product. If you can write an email describing what you need from a colleague, you have the core skill — the Circle teaches you to apply it to AI.

Which tools do I need? Do I have to buy new subscriptions?

No new subscriptions are required. You'll work with the AI access you already have — a workplace Copilot, an existing or free ChatGPT or Claude account, whatever is genuinely available to you. The program teaches capabilities before brands, and one of its explicit goals is to help you cut unnecessary spending on overlapping subscriptions, not add to it.

What if I'm anxious or skeptical about AI?

Then you're exactly who this was built for. AI adoption is emotional before it is technical, and the Circle treats anxiety as data, not weakness — Week 1 begins there deliberately. Skeptics make the room better; you'll never be asked to perform enthusiasm you don't feel.

What's the time commitment?

One two-hour live session per week for eight weeks, plus roughly one to two hours applying that week's practice to your own work. Because assignments run on work you already have to do, much of that time replaces effort rather than adding to it. Optional workflow clinics add up to four more hours across the program if you want them.

What if I miss a session?

Sessions are recorded (teaching and demonstration segments only — peer shares are never recorded, per the confidentiality agreements), and recordings are available to your cohort through the program and for 90 days after it ends. That said, this is a practice room, not a video library — plan to attend live for the large majority of sessions; the peer practice is where the change happens.

Is my work confidential?

Yes. The cohort operates under working agreements and confidentiality expectations that every participant signs, and the responsible-use curriculum includes clear rules about what should never enter a public AI tool — rules developed through licensed delivery in enterprise and healthcare settings. You'll practice on real work without exposing it.
